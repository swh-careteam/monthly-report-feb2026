§ 02Monthly Analysis
March 2026 was defined by critical voice SL failures across all POD queues and severe callback wait times, offset only by strong email handling and above-threshold chat answer rates.

Voice performance remained severely below target across all nine queues. The team answered only 1,159 of 2,477 voice contacts (46.8%), with an abandon rate of 20.6%. Not a single voice queue reached the 80% SL target. The best-performing queue was Direct Routing - Care Team at 59.1% SL, primarily due to its very short ASA (11 seconds) from direct routing mechanics. The four Care Experience POD Voice queues ranged from 7.1% (POD2) to 14.9% (POD1) SL — a consistent failure pattern seen across all three weeks with weekly reports.

Telemedica Team answered only 18.2% of its 330 offered contacts, abandoning 114 calls (34.5% abandon rate) and achieving just 13.8% SL. With an average speed of answer of 8 minutes 7 seconds, this queue continues to be the most critically understaffed. Callback wait times compound this issue: six of seven callback queues averaged over 2 hours, with Client Support callbacks averaging 10 hours 45 minutes — more than a full workday before return contact.

On the agent side, Miguel Garcia dominated throughput with 710 total answered contacts (415 voice, 133 email, 162 chat) — nearly double the next-highest agent, La'Shay Richard at 344. However, several agents flagged for extreme AHT anomalies: Shalon Jones logged an email AHT of 1 day 19 hours 45 minutes on just 6 interactions, and Tatiana Jean-Louis recorded 1 day 6 hours in chat ACW. Thia Decker's “Not Responding” status totaled 12 days 3 hours, which almost certainly represents a system or routing error requiring investigation.

§ 01Manager Action ItemsBased on March 2026 data
Urgent
Telemedica Team: Emergency Staffing Review
Telemedica answered only 60 of 330 offered calls (18.2% answer rate, 34.5% abandon rate) with an SL of just 13.8% in March. This pattern persisted across all 3 weekly reports (8.7%, 5.9%, 33.7% weekly SL). The queue had the longest ASA at 8m 7s and the highest abandon volume (114). Conduct an immediate workforce analysis: review scheduled vs. logged-in agent hours, examine the Telemedica CS/VA skill assignments, and determine if agent Alex DeTrolio (the only agent showing Telemedica skills with any activity) is the sole agent handling this queue.
Data: 330 offered · 60 answered · 114 abandoned · SL 13.8% · All 3 weeks below 34%
Urgent
Callback Wait Times Require Immediate SLA Policy
Six of seven callback queues averaged over 2 hours of wait time before callback placement. Client Support averaged 10h 45m and POD1 averaged 8h 31m — callers waiting over a full workday for a callback is a serious service failure. Establish and enforce an internal callback SLA target (recommended: 2 hours maximum). Assign dedicated daily callback processing windows for each queue supervisor, add callback queue depth to the daily standup dashboard, and consider same-day callback guarantees in agent scripting.
Data: Client Support 10h 45m · POD1 8h 31m · POD2 4h 10m · CE Team 3h 44m · 6/7 queues above 2h
Urgent
Chat ACW Crisis — All 4 POD Queues Flagged
Every chat queue exceeded the 15-minute ACW threshold by a wide margin. POD3 Chat averaged 4h 39m of ACW per interaction — nearly 19 times the threshold. Individual agent data shows Ashley Bradley at 5h 50m chat ACW, Auny Elias-Heath at 9h 59m, Rashidah Brown at 7h 20m, and Tatiana Jean-Louis at 1 day 6 hours. This suggests agents are consistently leaving chat interactions open in ACW state rather than closing them. Audit whether the chat wrap-up workflow is functioning correctly and whether agents understand how to properly disposition chat contacts.
Data: POD3 ACW 4h 39m · POD1 2h 19m · POD4 1h 33m · POD2 1h 03m · Target: <15m
High Priority
CE POD Voice Queues: Routing and Staffing Overhaul
All four Care Experience POD Voice queues failed to meet the 80% SL target in March, ranging from 7.1% (POD2) to 14.9% (POD1). Combined they received 682 calls and answered only 288 (42.2%). Meanwhile, Care Experience Team Voice handled 588 calls at 69.6% — suggesting the team-level queue is absorbing overflow effectively but the POD-specific routing is fundamentally broken. Review the routing strategy: consider whether POD voice queues should overflow to CE Team Voice sooner, or whether agents assigned to POD queues are also picking up chat/email at the same time.
Data: POD2 SL 7.1% · POD3 8.3% · POD4 12.2% · POD1 14.9% · Combined: 42.2% answer rate

Zero voice queues reached the 80% SL target in any week of March. Chat ACW: Worsening Pattern
Chat ACW exceeded 15 minutes across all four queues every week of the month. At the agent level, several individuals are accumulating ACW measured in hours or days. This suggests the issue is not random — it reflects either a systemic process failure (agents not knowing how to close chat properly) or an agent behavior issue that has not been corrected despite appearing in weekly reports.
POD3 Chat ACW: 4h 39m · POD1: 2h 19m · Target: <15m
👤
Agent Performance: High Concentration
Miguel Garcia alone accounted for 710 of the team's inbound answered contacts — approximately 33% of all inbound volume across all agents. The top 3 agents (Garcia, La'Shay Richard, Rashidah Brown) collectively handled over 1,280 contacts. This level of concentration creates risk: if any top-3 agent is absent, queue performance will deteriorate significantly. Cross-training and workload distribution deserve attention.
Top 3 agents: 1,286 of ~3,892 total contacts · 33% of volume from 1 agent
🕐
Callback Waits: Structural, Not Episodic
With 6 of 7 callback queues averaging over 2 hours wait, and Client Support at 10h 45m, this is not a one-day spike — it's a structural capacity problem. The weekly reports flagged callback waits in all three weeks. The completion rate is high (callbacks do get returned), but the 10-hour wait time makes the callback option effectively non-functional for callers expecting same-day return contact.
6/7 queues >2h avg wait · Client Support avg: 10h 45m all month

Three areas require progress before the April report: (1) Telemedica staffing resolution or routing change — the 13.8% monthly SL is unsustainable; (2) Chat ACW root-cause fix — whether system configuration or agent coaching; (3) Callback SLA policy formalization — internal targets must be established and measured weekly.
