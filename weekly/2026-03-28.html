§ 02 Weekly Analysis
This week shows a significant voice recovery alongside persistent structural issues — answer rates improved dramatically but Telemedica remains critical, chat ACW anomalies continue, and callback wait times remain unacceptable despite near-perfect completion.

Voice performance improved substantially this week. Of 437 contacts offered, 266 were answered (60.9%) — up 21.4 percentage points from 39.5% last week. The abandon rate dropped from 24.8% to 17.4%. However, only two queues (Client Support at 58.6% SL and Health Care Coordinators at 59.5% SL) exceeded the 50% SL threshold. All 8 queues still missed the 80% target.

Telemedica Team continues in crisis despite partial recovery. With 89 voice contacts offered and only 30 answered (33.7%), Telemedica improved from last week's 5.9% but remains critically below target. A 27.0% abandon rate means more than 1 in 4 callers hung up. The Telemedica callback queue also shows no entries this week — though unlike last week this absence may reflect reduced demand rather than a complete routing failure.

Chat ACW anomaly persists in POD3. POD3 Chat avg ACW of 2h 1m 43s remains far outside normal bounds (recommended: 3–5 minutes). POD2 Chat ACW of 32m 31s is also elevated. Both queues must be audited for stuck wrap-up states. POD4 Chat improved: ACW of 11m 45s is still elevated but the prior-week extreme of 2h 28m has resolved.
