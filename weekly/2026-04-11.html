§ 01 Key Findings & Recommendations Week of Apr 6–10, 2026
⚠ Critical — Voice
Client Support Answer Rate: 36.2%
Client Support (New) answered only 21 of 58 offered calls (36.2%), with 16 abandoned (27.6%). Service level dropped to 16.2%. This queue continues to significantly underperform relative to the 80% target. Consider immediate staffing review and potential callback enrollment for overflow contacts.
⚠ Critical — Voice SL
POD2 Voice Service Level: 9.7%
Care Exp. POD2 Voice had the lowest service level of any queue at 9.7%, meaning nearly all answered contacts waited beyond the threshold. With 40 offered and only 23 answered, this POD needs immediate attention to routing and staffing coverage during business hours.
△ Action Needed — Voice
Telemedica & HCC Below 60% Answer Rate
Telemedica Team answered 50.7% (38 of 75 offered) and Health Care Coordinators answered 56.8% (25 of 44). Both queues had abandon rates above 20%. Focus on scheduling optimization and consider dedicating additional agent capacity during peak intervals (10:30 AM–1:30 PM).
△ Action Needed — Chat SL
Chat Service Levels Below Target Across All PODs
All four chat queues fell well below the 80% service level target. POD2 Chat had the lowest at 26.4%, while POD3 Chat had the longest ASA at 40m 7s. Despite good answer rates (84–96%), contacts are waiting too long before being picked up.
△ Monitor — Callback Wait
Extended Callback Wait Times for CS & CE Team
Client Support callbacks waited an average of 9h 43m, and CE Team Voice callbacks averaged 7h 2m. While completion rates are strong (97–100%), the extended wait times indicate callers are queuing overnight or across shifts. Consider setting maximum callback window and overflow routing.
✓ Positive — Email & Callback
Email (98.8%) and Callback (100%) Performing Well
Email and callback channels continue to exceed targets. Email maintained 98.8% answer rate and callback reached 100% completion this week. POD3 and POD1 Email both achieved 100% service level, demonstrating strong asynchronous channel management.
✓ Positive — Agents
Miguel Garcia Led Team with 182 Answered Contacts
Miguel Garcia handled 182 total contacts across voice (84), email (25), and chat (73) — the highest volume on the team. La’Shay Richard (89) and Rashidah Brown (56) also showed strong multi-channel performance. 8 agents had zero activity and may warrant schedule review.
ℹ Week-over-Week
Volume Down vs. Prior Week, But SL Gaps Remain
Total voice offered dropped from 563 (week of Mar 29) to 412 this week, a 26.8% decrease. Answer rate improved slightly from 54.2% to 58.7%. However, service level gaps persist across all voice queues, suggesting insufficient staffing relative to demand even at lower volume.
§ 02 Recommended Actions This Week Prioritized by impact
  1. Client Support (New): Review agent scheduling and routing for this queue. Escalate if staffing is the root cause. Target 60%+ answer rate next week.
  2. POD2 Voice SL (9.7%): Investigate why contacts are not being answered within threshold. Review agent availability during POD2 peak hours (10:30 AM–1:00 PM).
  3. Chat SL Improvement: Consider reducing agent wrap-up (ACW) time to increase availability between chats. POD3 ACW is very high relative to talk time.
  4. Callback Wait Reduction: Review callback scheduling windows for CS and CE Team. Consider auto-expiry rules or same-day callback policies.
  5. Zero-Activity Agents (8): Confirm schedule compliance for agents with no answered contacts. Ensure they are properly rostered and accessible.
